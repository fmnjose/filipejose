Backend Developer at Crossjoin

My most recent work experience was as a backend developer at Crossjoin, a Portuguese consultancy focused on performance.

Here I worked with a telecommunications client, being part of the team responsible for investigating performance issues with the web portal that would be intensified on critical days such as Black Friday, Christmas and major smartphone releases.

Our work focused on analysing the codebase of several Spring Boot services to identify bottlenecks. We would then design and implement solutions to the problems, documenting every step of the process.

This was a wonderful opportunity to consolidate the Java knowledge I had gained at university through a real use case, and to extend it by learning Spring Boot and exploring topics such as VThreads, reactive programming (Webflux) and native image compilation (GraalVM). It was also a good opportunity to learn more about system design and Redis.

It was a great environment for personal development as the nature of the job incentivised me to do a lot of research into different technologies and analyse complex codebases.
